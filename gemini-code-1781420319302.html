What is CRO? o "What does CRO mean to you?"

Hacer el checkout más fácil para el usuario, lo que significa más ingresos para el negocio.
Well, CRO (Si - Ar - Ou) means Conversion Rate Optimization. The goal is using design to remove friction from the user journey so more people complete the main action. In short: in eCommerce, it means making the checkout easier for the user, which drives more revenue for the business.
How do you balance business goals with user needs (la teoría)

El objetivo supremo, lograr que ambos ganen. Experiencia fluida y simple el negocio vende y factura.
For me, the balance is through CRO. To help the business goals, the journey must be completely without friction. First, I focus on Conversion Rate by making the core flow simple for the user. Second, the idea is to reduce cart abandonment by designing a clean, easy checkout. And third, I increase Average Order Value by introducing smart upselling components at the right moment without frustrating the user. In short: if you make the experience great for the user, you drive revenue growth for the business.
How do you approach CRO and conversion-focused design (la practica)
▼
Product Designer Digital Designer Interview Mark
CRO (es el puente entre el negocio y el usuario) → Cart Abandonment → AOV, en ese orden, siempre.
For me, CRO (Si - Ar - Ou) design is about understanding that every visual decision must drive a business metric. I approach it using a clear hierarchy of three priorities, always in this specific order: 1. Conversion Rate (CVR): The goal here is to ensure the core user journey is completely without friction. If the user can't complete the main action easily, no other metric matters. 2. Cart Abandonment Rate: I focus on the checkout flow, making sure form fields are simplified, legal requirements are clear, and error states don't frustrate the user. 3. Average Order Value (AOV - Ei - Ou - Vi): Once the conversion flow is stable, I look for UI opportunities to introduce smart upselling or cross-selling components—like suggesting premium plate styles or accessories—at the right moment in the journey without disrupting the purchase. In short: First, increase conversion; second, lower cart abandonment; and third, raise the average spend.
