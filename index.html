Well, CRO (Ci - Ar - Ou) means Conversion Rate Optimization.

The goal here is to ensure the core user journey is as frictionless as possible.

In short: in eCommerce, it means making the checkout easier for the user, which drives more revenue for the business.

For me, CRO design is about connecting design decisions with business outcomes.

I approach it using a clear hierarchy of three priorities:

First, Conversion Rate (CVR). The goal here is to ensure the core user journey is completely without friction. If the user can't complete the main action easily, no other metric matters.

Second, Cart Abandonment Rate. I focus on the checkout flow, making sure form fields are simplified, legal requirements are clear, and error states don't frustrate the user.

And third, Average Order Value - AOV (Ei - Ou - Vi). Once the conversion flow is stable, I look for UI opportunities to introduce smart upselling or cross-selling components at the right moment in the journey without disrupting the purchase.

In short: First, increase conversion; second, lower cart abandonment; and third, raise the average spend.

1. CR / CVR (Conversion Rate)

Es la métrica principal. Si la web no convierte, ninguna otra métrica importa. Todo tu trabajo de UX/UI impacta directamente acá.

2. Cart Abandonment Rate

Es el cuello de botella más crítico. El usuario ya mostró intención de compra pero se trabó al final. Tu foco en el diseño del checkout es bajar este porcentaje.

3. AOV (Average Order Value)

Una vez que lográs que la web convierta de forma estable, el siguiente paso del negocio es que cada cliente gaste más plata en esa compra a través de componentes de Upselling o Cross-selling.

En resumen: Primero que compren (CR), segundo que no abandonen el carrito, y tercero que gasten más (AOV).

For me, the balance is through CRO.

And third, I increase Average Order Value by introducing smart upselling components at the right moment without frustrating the user.

In short: if you make the experience great for the user, you drive revenue growth for the business.
